## 按文章顺序修改

### 引言段

原句：

> machines, especcially robots, gradually take place of human labor

建议改为：

> machines, **especially** robots, **are increasingly taking the place of** human labor

这里有三个问题：

- **especcially → especially**：拼写错误。
- **take place of → take the place of**：固定搭配中不能缺少 **the**。
- **are increasingly taking** 比 **gradually take** 更符合“越来越多的工作被机器完成”的趋势。

原句：

> jobs and tasks which need hard physical work

建议改为：

> jobs and tasks **that involve strenuous physical work**

工作或任务本身不是“需要体力劳动”，而是“涉及体力劳动”。因此用 **involve** 更准确。**strenuous physical work** 也比 **hard physical work** 更正式。

原句：

> while such movement does cause some problems about employment to some extent

建议改为：

> while **this trend may cause some employment problems**

**movement** 通常表示社会运动、身体移动或特定活动，不适合指代题目中的发展趋势，应使用 **trend**。

**problems about employment** 搭配不自然，可以说：

- **employment problems**
- **job losses**
- **unemployment**

此外，**while、does、some、to some extent** 同时出现使句子显得冗长。强调结构 **does cause** 语法正确，但这里没有必要刻意强调。

### 个人影响段

原句：

> hard physical work always brings out irreversible body damage

建议改为：

> hard physical work **can cause long-term or even irreversible physical injuries**

主要问题：

- **always** 过于绝对。并非所有体力劳动都一定造成不可逆伤害，用 **can** 更客观。
- **bring out** 表示“使某种品质显现出来”，例如 _bring out the best in someone_，不能表示“造成伤害”。
- **body damage** 不自然，常用 **physical injuries** 或 **physical harm**。

原句：

> which will accompany workers during the rest of their life

建议改为：

> which **may affect workers for the rest of their lives**

**damage accompanies somebody** 是中文化表达。伤病通常用 **affect workers**。另外，主语是复数 **workers**，所以应为 **their lives**。

原句：

> It is beneficial for health and safety

建议改为：

> It is beneficial **to workers’ health and safety**

固定搭配通常是：

> be beneficial **to** something

而且需要明确是谁的健康和安全。

这一段的问题还在于论证较短。你提出了“保护健康和安全”，但没有例子。可以补充矿井、建筑工地、化工厂等危险环境，从而提升 Task Response。

### 企业影响段

原句：

> machines have various advantages in industry without any doubt

建议改为：

> machines **offer clear advantages to businesses**

**without any doubt** 比较口语化，也没有必要。雅思写作中最好直接陈述观点，而不是使用“毫无疑问”来加强语气。

原句：

> Depending on sophisticated programming

建议改为：

> **With sophisticated programming**

**depending on** 表示“取决于”，容易使读者理解为机器的优势取决于程序设计。这里想表达“凭借精密的程序设计”，用 **with** 更合适。

原句：

> machines have high pricision

建议改为：

> machines **can operate with high precision**

- **pricision → precision**：拼写错误。
- 英语通常不说机器“拥有高精度”，而说机器“以高精度运行”。

原句：

> work continuously for 7 days and 24 hours

建议改为：

> work continuously **24 hours a day, seven days a week**

或者：

> operate **around the clock**

**for 7 days and 24 hours** 容易被理解为总共工作“7天零24小时”。

原句：

> increase working efficiency significantly and reduce the expenditure on labor

建议改为：

> **significantly increase productivity and reduce labor costs**

- **working efficiency** 不算错误，但 **productivity** 更符合企业生产语境。
- **expenditure on labor** 可以理解，但 **labor costs** 更简洁自然。
- 副词 **significantly** 放在动词前通常更自然。

原句：

> update the machines, apply new technology, and expand productivity

建议改为：

> **upgrade automated systems, adopt new technologies, and expand production capacity**

搭配方面：

- upgrade machines/systems：升级设备或系统
- adopt technology：采用技术
- expand production capacity：扩大产能

**expand productivity** 不自然，因为 productivity 是效率或生产率，通常用 **increase productivity**，不能直接用 **expand**。

### 消极影响及反驳段

原句：

> the movement of fully automated industry

建议改为：

> **the transition towards automation**

原表达不自然。常见搭配是：

- the transition towards automation
- the development of automation
- the increasing use of automated systems

原句：

> decrease the available workplace

建议改为：

> **reduce the number of available jobs**

**workplace** 表示“工作场所”，例如办公室或工厂，而不是“工作岗位”。工作岗位应使用 **jobs、positions** 或 **job opportunities**。

原句：

> more and more contenders competing for a job

建议改为：

> more **job seekers competing for a limited number of positions**

**contender** 通常指比赛、竞选或竞争中的有力竞争者，不适合泛指求职者。

原句：

> this tendency is overwhelming

这里的 **overwhelming** 使用错误。它通常表示：

- 数量或程度极大的：overwhelming evidence
- 令人难以承受的：overwhelming pressure
- 压倒性的：an overwhelming majority

你想表达的是“这一趋势不可阻挡”，可以说：

> this trend is **inevitable**

或者：

> this trend is **unlikely to be reversed**

不过，“自动化不可阻挡”并不能直接证明其优点超过缺点。更有效的反驳方式是说明：

> 失业可以通过职业培训得到缓解，而且自动化也会创造编程、维修和工程领域的新岗位。

原句：

> be competitive with machines

建议改为：

> **remain competitive in the labor market**

人类一般不是与机器直接“竞争”，而是在自动化环境下保持就业竞争力。

原句：

> it is neccesary for people

应改为：

> it is **necessary** for people

**necessary** 的正确拼写是一个 **c**、两个 **s**。

原句：

> master high-level skills to manipulate machines and design the gaming rules

建议改为：

> **acquire advanced technical skills to operate, maintain, and design automated systems**

- **manipulate machines** 容易带有“操纵、控制”的负面或机械意味，使用 **operate machines** 更自然。
- **gaming rules** 指游戏规则，不能表示机器运行规则。
- 如果想表达“设计控制机器的规则”，可以使用 **design algorithms and control systems**。

### 结论段

原句：

> machines may result in more unemployment

建议改为：

> machines may **lead to unemployment in some sectors**

**result in more unemployment** 语法上可以，但表达较笼统。**in some sectors** 能避免过度概括。

原句：

> their benefits are large and conspicuous

建议改为：

> their benefits are **substantial and evident**

**conspicuous** 表示“非常显眼、引人注目”，例如：

> a conspicuous mistake  
> conspicuous consumption

它不常用来评价“好处很明显”。可以使用：

- substantial：重大的
- significant：显著的
- evident：明显的

原句：

> reshaping labor market

应改为：

> reshaping **the** labor market

**labor market** 在这里指整体劳动力市场，通常需要定冠词 **the**。

原句：

> which is irresistable

正确拼写是 **irresistible**。不过结尾只强调自动化“不可阻挡”，没有再次回答“优点是否超过缺点”。结论应明确比较：

> 在适当职业培训的帮助下，自动化在安全和生产效率方面的好处超过其就业风险。

Nowadays, machines, **especially** robots, **are increasingly taking the place of** human labor in jobs and tasks **that involve strenuous physical work**. From my perspective, while **this trend may cause some employment problems**, the positive effects **far outweigh its negative consequences**.

For individuals, hard physical work **can cause long-term or even irreversible physical injuries**, which **may affect workers for the rest of their lives**. It is **therefore beneficial to workers’ health and safety** to use machines to perform **difficult and dangerous** tasks. **For example, robots can operate in mines, construction sites, and factories where employees might otherwise be exposed to toxic substances, heavy loads, or serious accidents.**

For businesses, machines **offer clear advantages**. **With** sophisticated programming, machines **can operate with high precision and work continuously, 24 hours a day and seven days a week. This can significantly increase productivity and reduce labor costs.** Furthermore, **companies can upgrade automated systems, adopt new technologies, and expand production capacity more easily. As a result, businesses can manufacture products more efficiently while maintaining consistent quality.**

Admittedly, **the transition towards automation may reduce the number of available jobs** and cause a wave of unemployment, **particularly among low-skilled workers**. **It may also intensify competition for the remaining positions.** However, **this disadvantage can be mitigated through education and vocational training**. Rather than **competing directly with machines**, people can **acquire advanced technical skills to operate, maintain, and design automated systems**. **Moreover, automation can create new employment opportunities in areas such as programming, engineering, and equipment maintenance. Therefore, its negative effects are serious but not necessarily permanent.**

In conclusion, although machines may **lead to unemployment in some sectors**, their benefits **to worker safety, productivity, and product quality are more substantial**. **With appropriate retraining programs, society can reduce job displacement while continuing to benefit from automation.**
